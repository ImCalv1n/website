About Me

I’m studying computer science at the Binghamton University, SUNY undergraduate program. This past semester, I took coursework in data science and artificial intelligence. I did project work implementing web data collectors for toxicity analysis and implementing AI algorithms on the game Pac-Man. Outside of the classroom, I was the president of Binghamton’s chapter of the Theta Tau professional engineering fraternity where I acted as the main representative of the chapter to national and local organizations, appointed non-elected positions, and organized weekly meetings with the chapter, executive board, and alumni panel to set and track chapter plans and goals for the semester. I am now the Theta Tau North East Region Delegate to the Student Advisory Council where I represent the North East chapters of the national organization. I am also working on a research project in Binghamton's Autonomous Intelligent Robotics Lab, designing a real-world experiment to fine-tune an on-board force estimator for a quadruped robot. Having an interest in music, I spend some of my spare time playing lead trombone in the Harpur Jazz Ensemble on campus.
